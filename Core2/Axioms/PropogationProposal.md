**Draft Appendix: Propagation, Scattering, Coupling, and Ambient Tension**

1. **Tension Is Propagative**  
A tension state is not only a stored discrepancy. It may propagate through carriers, frames, and environments as an active signal.

2. **Tension Broadcast Is Not Addressed**  
A propagating tension does not target a specific object by name. It broadcasts its imbalance into the environment and may couple with any compatible structure that can reduce or absorb that imbalance.

3. **Propagation Carries Error, Not Instruction**  
A propagating packet does not inherently say what must change. It expresses an error state, pressure, mismatch, or unresolved tendency. The receiving structure determines what response, if any, lowers total tension.

4. **Carriers Guide Propagation**  
A carrier is any structure along which tension may propagate. A carrier may be geometric, algebraic, procedural, symbolic, or topological.

5. **Longitudinal And Transverse Aspects**  
Propagation has at least two aspects:
- longitudinal propagation along a carrier
- transverse coupling across carriers, fields, or neighboring structures

6. **Carrier State Matters**  
Propagation depends on carrier state, including orientation, polarity, phase, internal history, and current continuation law.

7. **Frames May Differ**  
The visible carrier frame, the propagation frame, and the ambient frame need not be identical. A local carrier may lie inside a larger propagation space.

8. **Boundary Events Are Scattering Events**  
When a propagating tension reaches a boundary, junction, or frame transition, the incident packet is transformed into one or more outgoing packets. This is a scattering event.

9. **Scattering Produces A Branch Family**  
A scattering event may yield several lawful outgoing continuations. These outgoing packets form a branch family.

10. **Reflection Is A Response Mode**  
Reflection is not a primitive force by itself. It is the response mode in which incident tension is returned along the same or an equivalent carrier with reversed longitudinal orientation.

11. **Wrap Is A Response Mode**  
Wrap is not merely a jump. It is boundary identification or periodic topological equivalence. Incident tension leaving one boundary may lawfully re-enter through an identified continuation.

12. **Continuation Is A Response Mode**  
Continuation is the response mode in which incident tension leaves the local carrier and persists into a larger propagation frame or ambient environment.

13. **Clamp And Dissipation Are Response Modes**  
A frame may absorb, damp, or locally trap incident tension. Clamp and dissipation are lawful response modes, not failures.

14. **Split Is A Response Mode**  
Split occurs when one incident propagation cannot be carried on a single continuation and is scattered into several outgoing continuations.

15. **Response Modes May Be Mixed**  
A boundary or junction need not be purely reflective, purely periodic, or purely continuous. It may distribute incident tension among several response modes, asymmetrically if needed.

16. **Asymmetry Is Lawful**  
Forward and reverse propagation need not behave identically. A carrier or boundary may transmit, reflect, wrap, or dissipate differently by direction, polarity, phase, or frame relation.

17. **Midpoints And Internal Landmarks May Emerge**  
Reflective and resonant propagation may create preferred internal locations such as midpoints, nodes, antinodes, pivots, or split sites. These need not be imposed externally.

18. **Coupling Is Tension Exchange**  
Attraction, repulsion, alignment, joining, divergence, and stopping are coupling effects produced when propagating tensions interact with other carriers, fields, or frames.

19. **Attraction Lowers Separation Tension**  
Attraction occurs when reducing separation, mismatch, or displacement lowers total tension.

20. **Repulsion Lowers Overlap Or Crowding Tension**  
Repulsion occurs when increasing separation lowers total tension.

21. **Alignment Lowers Angular Or Role Mismatch**  
Alignment is distinct from attraction. Two structures may align without approaching each other if angular mismatch is the active tension.

22. **Joining Is A Capture Event**  
A join occurs when compatible propagating structures enter a regime where unified continuation lowers total tension more than separate continuation.

23. **Crossing Does Not Imply Joining**  
Geometric intersection alone is not sufficient for a join. Crossing is spatial coincidence; joining is topological capture.

24. **Stopping Is Tension-Relative**  
A propagation may stop because:
- tension is sufficiently resolved
- a terminal law is reached
- available continuation falls below threshold
- dissipation exceeds transmission
- a stronger competing response captures the packet

25. **Pliability Determines What Changes**  
A propagated tension may change the carrier, the boundary, the ambient frame, a neighboring object, or a junction relation. What changes depends on which structure is pliable under that tension.

26. **Environment Is Reactive**  
The environment is not a passive container. It may absorb, amplify, redirect, or reshape propagation, and may itself emit secondary tensions in response.

27. **Objects May Emit Into Environment**  
A structure may reduce local tension by exporting it into the environment. This does not require direct communication with a specific target.

28. **Ambient Tensions May Merge**  
Independent propagating tensions may superpose, reinforce, cancel, or combine into new effective tensions not explicitly present in the original local sources.

29. **Emergent Resolution Is Lawful**  
A resolved pattern need not be explicitly encoded in any one source. It may emerge from interaction among many propagated tensions and environmental responses.

30. **Local Reduction, Global Tradeoff**  
A response that lowers one local tension may raise another elsewhere. Dynamic resolution seeks lawful balance, not guaranteed local perfection.

31. **Coupling Need Not Be Geometric**  
These laws apply not only to geometry. They may also govern control flow, program loops, symbolic systems, markets, social systems, conceptual graphs, or any domain where tensions propagate and couple.

32. **Programmatic Interpretation**  
In a procedural interpretation, a loop, branch, retry, merge, timeout, or interrupt may be treated as a propagation-and-scattering response rather than as a separate unrelated kind of event.

33. **Geometric Interpretation**  
In a geometric interpretation, growth, branching, joining, spacing, and landmark alignment may be treated as the visible trace of propagated and coupled tensions.

34. **Final Propagation Principle**  
The primitive executable content is not a fixed instruction to one target, but a propagating tension seeking lawful reduction across carriers, frames, and environments. Reflection, wrap, continuation, split, join, and related behaviors are structured response modes of that more general process.

**Short Glossary**

- **Propagating tension**: an active imbalance moving through the system.
- **Carrier**: a structure that guides propagation.
- **Scattering event**: a boundary or junction response that converts one incident packet into one or more outgoing packets.
- **Ambient frame**: the larger environment into which tension may leak or continue.
- **Coupling**: lawful interaction between propagated tensions and other structures.
- **Capture**: a transition from separate continuation to joined continuation.
- **Pliability**: how able a structure is to change under active tension.
